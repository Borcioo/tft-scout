## Observations

1. **`phaseCompanionSeeded` is the primary target.** At ~12 s per locked
   scenario it alone consumes more than the entire target budget. Either its
   attempt count scales badly with the candidate pool, or it re-runs expensive
   inner work per iteration. A targeted fix here is the single biggest lever
   we have.
2. **`phaseTemperatureSweep` is the secondary target.** 5 s per locked run
   and 1.4 s even on the no-lock scenario. Its `attempts = Math.max(maxResults * 3, 60)`
   multiplier scales with `maxResults`, which for locked cases is 360 — so it
   runs ~1 080 `buildOneTeam` iterations. Reducing the multiplier or skipping
   the phase entirely when `lockedTraits.length > 0` is the obvious move.
3. **`engine.enrichLoop` and its sub-spans are NOT the bottleneck.** All three
   combined (`buildActiveTraits`, `teamScore`, `teamScoreBreakdown`) total
   under 100 ms even on the heaviest scenario. The full scorer is fine —
   prior assumption that re-scoring 1 000+ teams was a hot spot is wrong.
4. **`engine.buildGraph` is trivial** at 2–4 ms. Caching it would buy us
   nothing.
5. **Hot-loop characterisation: `companionSeeded` alone can plausibly hit
   the 2 s target on locked scenarios if its work is cut by ~12×.** That is
   aggressive but the shape of the data suggests the phase is doing an
   `O(pool² · companions)` traversal or similar quadratic pattern; we'll
   know after reading its body in Phase B.
6. **`phaseLockedTraitSeeded`, the new locked-trait phase from the previous
   sprint, is cheap** (77–258 ms per locked run). It is doing its job
   efficiently — this is not where the fix needs to land.
